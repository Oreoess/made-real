## One-line

Made-Real is an independent digital venture design consultancy helping founders
and innovation teams move from fuzzy idea to validated direction — fast.

## What we actually do

Three fixed-price sprint packages under **The Realness Signal** methodology:

- **Real Problem** — problem-solution fit discovery
- **Real Product** — shaping & prototyping toward a testable build
- **Real Performance** — launch readiness & learning loops

Methodology phases: **Sense → Frame → Shape → Make → Learn & Evolve.**

Outputs are tangible: prototypes, MVPs, decision-ready artifacts. Not decks
for their own sake.

## Who we're for

- Early-stage founders post-idea, pre-PMF, who need to de-risk direction
- Product & innovation teams stuck in the fuzzy back end of a new bet
- Enterprise innovation leaders who need outside clarity without a big-consultancy footprint

**Not for:** staff-aug / pure execution work, pitch-deck-only engagements,
late-stage growth optimisation, or "just make it pretty" design briefs.

## Positioning

Made-Real sits between strategy consultancies (too abstract, too slow, too
expensive) and design/build agencies (too executional, start too late in the
arc). The wedge is **upstream clarity in the AI era** — where generative tools
let teams build the wrong thing faster than ever.

We call this "The Fuzzy Back End": AI has collapsed execution cost, so the
bottleneck has moved upstream to problem definition and judgement. That's
where Made-Real works.

## Point of view

- Problem-solution fit precedes product-market fit; most teams skip it and pay for it later.
- AI collapses execution cost, which makes problem clarity — not velocity — the new bottleneck.
- Taste, judgement and framing are the last defensible skills. Everything downstream of them is increasingly commoditised.
- Prototypes beat documents. Evidence beats opinion. Shipped beats perfect.
- Independent consultancies should behave more like studios than agencies — fewer, better engagements.

## How to help me

- **LinkedIn posts:** write in my natural voice, not generic thought-leader. Concrete, practitioner-tone, no "here are 5 lessons" cadence.
- **Client deliverables:** client-ready formatting. No placeholder fluff, no lorem ipsum, no "insert stat here" unless I've asked for a skeleton.
- **Strategy questions:** give me options with trade-offs, not one answer. I'll pick.
- **Drafting:** default to multiple versions (usually 2–3) with a one-line rationale for each.
- **Scope:** ask before expanding. If I ask for a tweet, don't hand me an essay.
- **Frameworks:** I like structured, measurable ones (problem-solution fit, discovery velocity, launch readiness). Lean into that.

## What to push back on

- Vague briefs — ask one or two clarifying questions before drafting
- Generic SaaS/startup advice that ignores the independent-consultancy context
- Anything that sounds like a 2015 agency deck or a 2022 LinkedIn carousel
- Suggesting I add headcount or build a big agency — that's not the direction
